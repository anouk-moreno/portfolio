Permwatch (BSc Thesis Project)

Android research app that analyzes installed applications and their declared/granted permissions, then runs a 7-day study asking users about 3 apps per day (high/low/medium usage) and 10 selected permissions per app, prioritizing risky permission combinations (PermPairs) based on prior research.

  • Status: Completed
  • Code: Private (university research project)
  • Platform: Android
  • Backend: Firebase Realtime Database
  • Key APIs: PackageManager, Usage Stats / Usage Access, Android permissions model

What it does

  • Phone scan: collects installed apps and declared permissions.
  • Daily survey: 3 apps/day selected by usage frequency bucket.
  • Permission sampling: 10 permissions/app prioritizing risky PermPairs.
  • Final analysis of apps containing top risky permission combinations.

Notes

  • Repository cannot be shared publicly due to academic privacy constraints.
