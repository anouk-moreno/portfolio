About

Computer Science graduate focused on cybersecurity and financially relevant systems.

Hi, my name is Anouk. I studied Computer Science with a specialization in Statistics at LMU Munich, Germany. Throughout my degree, I was exposed to a wide range of topics related to software development, computer architecture, networks, linear models, and introductory concepts in cybersecurity.

I would not say that I have deep expertise in cybersecurity yet. However, it is an area that has gradually captured my interest, particularly when applied to user security and real-world systems. At the same time, I am also interested in finance and in problems that lie at the intersection of technology, data, and economics. For this reason, I am currently exploring challenges that combine these domains and trying to understand how they interact in practice.

During my studies, I had the opportunity to work on applied projects, including my Bachelor’s thesis, which focused on user awareness and mobile permission analysis through a longitudinal study. While this project leaned more towards human–computer interaction than I initially expected, it helped me understand how small technical decisions, data collection choices, and human behavior can significantly affect real-world outcomes.

I am still at an early stage in defining my long-term professional direction, but for now I am focusing on building projects that allow me to explore both cybersecurity-related topics and economically relevant systems. Technical details about my work can be found in the Projects section of this portfolio.

On a more personal note, I plan to add access to my blog in the future, where I will share reflections and interests beyond purely technical topics. A detailed overview of my academic background and experience can be found in my CV, available on this page. For any further questions, feel free to reach out through the Contact section.
